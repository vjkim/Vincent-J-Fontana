What is Child Abuse?

    Child Abuse occurs when:

  • a parent or caregiver inflicts serious physical injury other than by accidental means.

    • This can occur in many forms. This includes

  • Neglect
  • failing to provide education, food, or shelter for children.
  • failing to be there for children when they need it most
  • Physical Abuse
  • Shaking
  • Beating
  • Biting
  • Kicking
  • Punching
  • Burning
  • Sexual Abuse
  • exposing a child to pornographic material or sexual activity
  • having a child pose, undress, or preform in a sexual manner
  • rape
  • attempted rape
  • sodomy
  • Exploitation
  • Emotional Abuse

How can we prevent child abuse from occuring?

  • We can pass laws preventing corporal punishment and child abuse.
  • We can try to stop it in our own households.
  • We can listen and believe children who disclose to us that they are being abused.
  • We can try to help those who are current victims of child abuse.
  • We can validate their experiences.
  • We need to make sure they feel heard.
