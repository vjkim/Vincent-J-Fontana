What is Child Abuse?

    Child Abuse occurs when a parent or caregiver inflicts serious physical injury other than by accidental means. This includes:

  • Neglect
    • Failing to provide education, food, or shelter for children.
    • Failing to be there for children when they need it most
  • Physical Abuse
    • Shaking
    • Beating
    • Biting
    • Kicking
    • Punching
    • Burning
  • Sexual Abuse
    • Exposing a child to pornographic material or sexual activity
    • Having a child pose, undress, or preform in a sexual manner
    • Rape
    • Attempted rape
    • Sodomy
  • Exploitation
  • Emotional Abuse

How can we prevent child abuse from occuring?

  • We can pass laws preventing corporal punishment and child abuse.
  • We can try to stop it in our own households.
  • We can listen and believe children who disclose to us that they are being abused.
  • We can try to help those who are current victims of child abuse.
  • We can validate their experiences.
  • We need to make sure they feel heard.

Sexual abuse is contact or interaction between a child and an adult when the child is being used for sexual stimulation of the perpetrator, or another person when the perpetrator, or another person is in a position of power or control over the victim.

  • Sexual abuse can be as minimal as:
    • exposing a child to pornographic materials and/or adult sexual activity,
    • having a child pose, undress, or perform in a sexual fashion
  • It can also be as severe as:
    • Rape, attempted rape,or sodomy

How can we help a child who has disclosed that he or she has had sexual abuse?

  • Belief is key. The child is only telling you because they trust you enough to tell you.
  • How can a kid tell you they've had as traumatic an experience as sexual abuse? Do you really think they'd make up such a thing as that?
  • Don't act surprised or mad in front of the child, it will only suppress their emotions and make it exen harder for them to cope with the experience.
  • Be there for your child or friend.
  • Let them know how much they are worth, and that their experience doesn't define them.
